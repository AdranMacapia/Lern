About Pasay City National Highschool

The institution traces its roots to the Philippine Air Force College of Aeronautics (PAFCA), which began as a barrio high school in 1966 at Basa Air Base, Pampanga. Initially, it offered the first three years of secondary education with an enrollment of 625 students. In 1967, it expanded to offer the complete four-year secondary education. The school was primarily established to cater to the dependents of Philippine Air Force personnel. The late Col. Norberto P. Furagganan of the Judge Advocate General Office, Philippine Air Force, is credited as the founder of this institution.

  • Vision - Mission:To protect and promote the right of every Filipino to quality, equitable, culture-based, and complete basic education where: Students learn in a child-friendly, gender-sensitive, safe, and motivating environment. Teachers facilitate learning and constantly nurture every learner.
  • Our Core Values: Maka-Diyos (God-fearing) Makatao (Humane) Makakalikasan (Environmentally Conscious) Makabansa (Patriotic)
