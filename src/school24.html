About St. Mary's Academy of Pasay

Established in 1922 as "Escuela Catolica de Pasay" by Rev. Fr. Egmidio Trinidad, the school initially offered primary education and was managed by the parish priest. In 1939, administration was transferred to the Religious of the Virgin Mary (RVM) upon the request of the Archbishop of Manila, Most Rev. Michael O’Doherty. The following year, it was renamed St. Mary's Academy to honor the Blessed Virgin Mary. The first superior under RVM administration was Mother Maria Josefa Avendaño.

  • Vision - Mission: Vision: St. Mary's Academy of Pasay City is an Ignacian Marian School committed to providing quality Catholic education for the development of persons, with a solid foundation in Christian faith and readiness to serve the Church and society. Mission: The institution aims to be an educational establishment that efficiently and effectively: Produces academically competent graduates, Provides client-focused services, Adopts quality management systems, Advocates community and Church services.

Available Strands

  • Humanities and Social Sciences (HUMSS) Strand.
  • Accountancy, Business, and Management (ABM) Strand.
