About University of the Philippines (Taguig Branch)

On June 18, 1908, a hundred and sixteen years ago, the University of the Philippines (UP) was founded through Act No. 1870 of the Philippine Assembly. UP was the result of the recommendation of Secretary of Public Instruction, William Morgan Shuster to the Philippine Commission, the upper house of the Philippine Assembly. It was meant to fill the need to meet the increasing demands for instruction in the higher levels of learning and to provide professional studies in medicine, law, engineering or applied sciences.

  • Juris Doctor (J.D.) program: Students must complete all courses and earn a general weighted average of at least 2.75 to graduate.
  • Practicum: Senior students must complete eight units of the practicum component and submit a publishable paper.
  • All courses in residence: Students must take all courses in residence.

Academic Units

The following units will offer graduate-level courses, continuing education programs, seminars, workshops, and training courses:

  • College of Law
  • College of Engineering
  • Virata School of Business
  • College of Architecture
  • School of Labor and Industrial Relations
  • School of Urban and Regional Planning
  • School of Statistics
  • UP Open University
