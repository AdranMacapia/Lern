About Miriam College

Miriam College was founded in 1926 as Malabon Normal School by the Maryknoll Sisters to train women teachers in the Philippines. It moved to Quezon City in 1952 and was renamed Maryknoll College. In 1977, ownership was transferred to lay administrators, leading to it's transformation into Miriam College. The school has since thrived, upholding values of truth, justice, and peace.

  • Rules: Miriam College values truth, equality, peace, and environmental stewardship. We commit to honest intellectual pursuits, promoting equality and social progress, fostering peace through non-violence, and protecting the environment for future generations through sustainable practices.
