About Lyceum of the Philippines University

Founder and Background: Lyceum of the Philippines University (LPU) was founded in 1952 by Dr. José P. Laurel, the third President of the Philippines. Named after the Lyceum of Aristotle in Greece, the university was established to provide a haven for academic excellence and produce graduates who embody the ideals of leadership, integrity, and service to the nation. Its main campus is located in Intramuros, Manila, with several campuses across the Philippines.

  • Vision - Mission: Vision: LPU envisions itself as an internationally recognized institution of higher learning, dedicated to innovation and excellence. Mission: The university aims to develop individuals who exemplify the ideals of truth and service, equipped with relevant skills and values for global competitiveness.
